Philip Emeagwali

A Nigerian-American Computer Scientist

Philip Emeagwali won the Gordon Bell Prize for high-performance computing applications, in an oil reservior modeling calculation using a novel mathematical formulation and implementation.

Philip Emeagwali's Biography

  • 1954 - Born in Akure, a SouthWestern state in Nigeria, Africa.
  • 1967 - Early School suspended due to the Nigerian Civil War where he was also a Biafran Army at age 13 link.
  • 1977 - Earned a bachelor's degree in Mathematics from Oregon State University through a scholarship after completing a correspondence course of the University of London.
  • 1986 - Master's degree from George Washington University in ocean and marine ocean and marine engineering and a second master's in Applied Mathematics from the University of Maryland.
  • 1989 - Golden Bell Prize for an application of the CM-2 massivelly-parallel computer. It is used Mobile Research and thimking for seismic data processing and achieved the higher ratio of 500 Mflops/$1M. This method involves each microprocessor communicating with six neighbors. His simulation was the first program to apply pseudo-time approach to reservoir modelling.
